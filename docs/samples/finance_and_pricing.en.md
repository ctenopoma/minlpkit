# Finance & Pricing

Portfolio selection, pricing, and revenue management. Includes nonlinear and discrete decisions such as risk terms (variance) and piecewise pricing.

**9 models** / 5 models supporting the `scale` argument. The `scale` column ✓ means the scale is adjustable via `build_model(scale=...)`.

| cash_flow_matching | Cash Flow Matching Problem. — A problem where an asset manager of a pension fund or insurance company decides how to "dedicate" future fixed liability payments (pension benefits, insurance payouts, etc.) per maturity period by investing in a portfolio of candidate bonds (coupon bonds) they hold. Each bond pays a coupon (interest) in each period until maturity, and the principal at maturity. | — | [source](https://github.com/ctenopoma/minlpkit/blob/main/samples/finance_and_pricing/cash_flow_matching.py) |
| credit_scoring_tree | Credit Scoring Threshold Optimization for risk evaluation. — The credit examination department of a consumer finance company decides the score cutoff to sort applicants into "approve/reject". Lowering the cutoff increases the number of approvals (= interest income) but also approves applicants with a high default risk. Because risk characteristics differ by age group (young/senior), separate cutoffs are optimized for each segment. | — | [source](https://github.com/ctenopoma/minlpkit/blob/main/samples/finance_and_pricing/credit_scoring_tree.py) |
| dynamic_pricing_hotel | Dynamic Pricing for Hotel Rooms. — A hotel revenue manager determines the selling price for multiple room types (standard, suite, etc.) for each day-of-week category (weekday, weekend, busy day). Raising the price increases the unit price but decreases the number of reservations along a price-elastic demand curve, making the revenue (price × demand) a nonlinear (quadratic) function of price. In addition to the physical capacity constraint of room inventory, it reflects revenue management business rules (price ordering constraints) such as setting higher prices for weekends and busy days than for weekdays. | — | [source](https://github.com/ctenopoma/minlpkit/blob/main/samples/finance_and_pricing/dynamic_pricing_hotel.py) |

| portfolio_mip | Portfolio Optimization with Cardinality Constraints. — A problem where a portfolio manager of an asset management company decides the investment allocation to maximize expected returns from among multiple candidate assets (equities, bonds, REITs, etc.) while adhering to an upper limit (cardinality) on the number of assets they can actually manage and monitor. | — | [source](https://github.com/ctenopoma/minlpkit/blob/main/samples/finance_and_pricing/portfolio_mip.py) |

Additionally, some projects have technical prerequisites (subsequent phases cannot be started unless investment in prior research is completed). The committee decides which group of projects to adopt (0/1 adoption decision) under a limited annual budget to maximize the sum of expected returns. | ✓ | [source](https://github.com/ctenopoma/minlpkit/blob/main/samples/finance_and_pricing/r_and_d_project_portfolio.py) |

Deeper markdowns increase demand but squeeze margins, and there is an operational rule in the field that once a category starts being marked down, the markdown amount must be maintained or expanded in subsequent weeks (because reversing the markdown damages customer trust). | ✓ | [source](https://github.com/ctenopoma/minlpkit/blob/main/samples/finance_and_pricing/retail_markdown_clearance.py) |
