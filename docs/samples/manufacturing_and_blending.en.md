# Manufacturing and Blending

Formulation, pooling, and foundry charging. Batch/multi-period models of process industries centered on the bilinear of concentration x flow rate (pooling).

**10 Models** (including 2 with particularly detailed business stories ⭐) / 4 models support the `scale` argument. A ✓ in the `scale` column means the scale can be varied with `build_model(scale=...)`.

| foundry_charge_mix | Foundry Charge Mix (Single-period, multi-order raw material blending) — A melter at a foundry determines how much of multiple scrap lots on hand (differing in carbon and copper content rates and inventory amounts) to blend and melt for one heat (melting in an electric arc furnace), the integer number of heats, and the continuous charge amount per time. Since one melted pool of metal is allocated to multiple orders, the components (concentration) of the molten metal must simultaneously satisfy the standard windows of all destination orders (bilinear constraint of concentration x allocation amount), and the portion that cannot meet the standards is compensated by relatively expensive external purchases (outsourcing standard-compliant materials). | — | [source](https://github.com/ctenopoma/minlpkit/blob/main/samples/manufacturing_and_blending/foundry_charge_mix.py) |

| semiconductor_wafer_fab | Semiconductor Wafer Fab Routing — A decision by a "transfer system manager" of a semiconductor manufacturing line to allocate wafer carriers (lots) to automated guided vehicles (OHT/AMR) that transport them between processes. Since the travel time from the current location to the origin of the lot differs for each robot, the total turnaround time until completion of transfer changes depending on the assignment. | ✓ | [source](https://github.com/ctenopoma/minlpkit/blob/main/samples/manufacturing_and_blending/semiconductor_wafer_fab.py) |
| steel_continuous_casting | Steel Continuous Casting Scheduling — A decision by a "casting process scheduler" of a steelworks to determine the input order and start time of multiple heats (molten steel batches) continuously cast on multiple casting lines (strands). Since continuous casting cannot empty the tundish (intermediate vessel), the minimum and maximum input interval (casting gap) between successive heats on the same line is determined by operations (if the interval is too short, solidification cannot catch up, and if it is too long, the molten steel in the tundish cools and causes quality defects). | ✓ | [source](https://github.com/ctenopoma/minlpkit/blob/main/samples/manufacturing_and_blending/steel_continuous_casting.py) |
